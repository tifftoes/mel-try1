P
Searing Brilliance
Passive Stack Execution System

Mel's passive allows her to stack essence on enemy champions. These stacks increase an enemy's execute threshold. This threshold can be seen by a mark over the head of the enemy and a small bar on their HP. Lowkey just watch the video below to get the hang of it.

Key Tips

  • Stack management is essential for ultimate timing
  • Passive stacks enable execute threshold indicators
  • Multiple abilities can apply stacks efficiently
Q
Radiant Volley
Primary Poke & Stack Tool

Mel's Q is a long-range ability that fires a series of missiles. It functions like Kai'Sa Q, but it's an untargeted skillshot.

Keynote

You will almost always hit 1 missile, but hitting all the missiles is tricky. Look to combo this spell with your E or your teammates' CC abilities.

How to Use

This ability is your bread and butter. It's an excellent poke tool and can provide you with tons of stacks for your passive. Importantly though, do not always worry about hitting all of the projectiles. This ability is on a short cooldown and can be cycled very quickly. Honestly, you only need to hit 1 missile to get good poke and proc your Scorch/Comet or First Strike. No need to be perfect here — just aim it reasonably well and look to abuse the enemy on-cooldown.

Key Tips

  • Cast on cooldown for consistent passive stacks
  • Combo with E or teammate CC for full missile hits
  • Focus on 1–2 missile hits rather than perfection
W
Barrier / Reflect
Most Complicated Ability in the Game

Mel's W puts up a barrier for 0.75 seconds that grants her a burst of movespeed, reflects enemy projectiles, and prevents her from taking damage from all sources.

Keynote

This is one of the most complicated abilities in the game. Firstly, the W will reflect spells back to their source at the point of reflection — the enemy still has time to dodge it, especially for long-range reflections.

Mel does not need to be the target of the spell to reflect it. If Mel walks in front of a Miss Fortune Q aimed at a teammate, the spell will still reflect back to hit Miss Fortune. And because Miss Fortune Q is a targeted spell, it is undodgeable as a reflection — targeted spells always hit the person who cast them.

Now, what will be reflected? Anything that Yasuo and Braum can block is considered a projectile. As a rule of thumb, anything fired horizontally — Veigar Baleful Strike, Katarina Q, Senna W — will be reflected. Anything shot from the ground or sky — Xerath R, Pantheon R, Veigar W — will not be reflected, though the damage will still be mitigated.

Key Tips

  • Can intercept spells aimed at teammates
  • Targeted spells always hit their caster when reflected
  • Very long cooldown (~35 seconds) — use wisely
E
Solar Snare
Root / Slow — Passive Stack Engine

Mel's E is a black hole that travels in a straight line for a set distance (this distance does not change). Enemies in the center of the hole will take extra damage and be rooted. Enemies hit by any part of the hole will take a small amount of damage and stack Mel's passive.

Keynote

Mel's E is an excellent source of passive stacking. It's important to note just how many stacks we can get here. In the case that we use Mel's E and the center hits the enemy champion, we will proc around 7 stacks. However, the hole does not stop after hitting a champion — if we managed to hit the enemy at the end of its range, rooting them in the process, we can get upwards of 10–12 stacks.

How to Use

Mel's E can be hard to hit and will almost never result in rooting the enemy when cast from long range. Instead, begin auto attacking the enemy champion, only using E after the enemy has attempted to trade back, luring them into closer combat. If we use our E effectively and root the enemy champion, combined with the safety of having our W as a defensive spell, almost no champion in the early game can outtrade us. The only fallback is that our W is on a really long cooldown (~35 seconds). When W is on cooldown, be careful using E aggressively — failing to land the root can leave us open to a counter-engage from the enemy laner.

Key Tips

  • Use after enemy commits to trade, not as opener
  • ~7 stacks for center hit, 10–12 stacks at max range root
  • Don't use aggressively when W is on cooldown
R
Golden Eclipse
Wait for the Golden Glow

Mel's ultimate does damage to all enemies with her passive applied to them. This damage is a flat number that increases slightly per the number of passive stacks applied to the enemy.

Keynote

When an enemy is in our ultimate execute threshold, they will have a glowing icon over their heads. Additionally, our ultimate HUD icon will start glowing golden. Outside of very rare circumstances where you want to explode your ultimate early to avoid your stacks expiring, you will typically want to wait for these indicators before casting this spell.

Key Tips

  • Wait for glowing icon indicators before casting
  • Ultimate HUD icon glows golden when enemy is in execute range

Combos

Core trading sequences — W is situational and not included; weave it in based on the matchup
Bread & Butter — E into Q
E→Q

Use E first for the slow/root to guarantee more Q missiles connect. This is the core trading pattern for Mel in virtually every lane.

E + Flash Extension Combo
E→Flash→Q

Flash during the cast-time of E to reposition mid-animation — similar to Ahri E + Flash. Use to surprise the enemy or extend E's effective range.

Maximum DPS — Auto Weave Combo
E→Auto→Q→Auto→R

Weave auto attacks between spells for maximum DPS. Most effective against melee champions who can't trade back at auto range. Against other mages, this brings you into their spell range — use with caution.

Note on W in combos: Mel does not have many rigid combos because W usage is entirely matchup-dependent. None of the above combos include W — but you should weave it in whenever you need to block or reflect the enemy's key ability. W is a reactive tool, not a fixed combo step.
